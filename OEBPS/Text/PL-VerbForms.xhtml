Verb forms (1)

εὕρηκα

(Archimedes in Plutarch's Moralia 1094c)

(I've found it)

-κα: perfect active, 1 pers. sg.

p. 1 of 3

ὣς ἔφατ᾽

(Hom. Il. 1.43)

(So he said)

ἔφατ᾽: contracted form of ἔφατο, imperfect middle 3rd sg of the verb φημί ("I say, I speak"). The prefix ἔ- (called "augment") indicates that the verb is in the past, the suffix -το is the ending used for 3rd person sg in the historical tenses/aspects and middle voice.

There is a debate whether the augment was originally a separate particle, of if it started since the beginning as a past tense marker prefixed to the verb (Willi 2018, Origins of the Greek verb, ch.7, CUP).

ἢν ἐθέλῃσιν

(Hom. Il. 9.429)

("if he wishes")

ἢν : "if" + SUBJUNCTIVE (whereas εἰ is foll.owed by ind. or opt.). Epic form also εἴ κε, or αἴ κε.

ἐθέλῃσιν: "he wished" (verb 3rd sg pres subj act epic nu_movable), from ἐθέλω ("to will, wish, purpose")

Κάλχας Θεστορίδης οἰωνοπόλων ὄχ᾽ ἄριστος,
ὃς ᾔδη τά τ᾽ ἐόντα τά τ᾽ ἐσσόμενα πρό τ᾽ ἐόντα

(Hom. Il. 1.69-70)

(Calchas son of Thestor, far the best of bird-diviners,
who knew the things that were, and that were to be, and that had been before)

ᾔδη : "knew", verb 3rd sg plup ind act doric aeolic contr

ἐόντα : "that were", part pl pres act neut acc epic doric ionic

ἐσσόμενα : "that will be", part pl fut mid neut acc epic

τ᾽ .. τ᾽ ..: (contraction of τε.. τε..) "both .. and .."
