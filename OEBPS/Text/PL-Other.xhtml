ἑκὰς ἀνδρῶν ἀλφηστάων

(Hom. Od. 6.8)

(far from men that live by toil)

-ων: GEN pl;
ἑκὰς + GEN: "far from";
ἀνδρῶν: "men";
ἀλφηστάων: industrious, enterprising. Originally might have literally meant "one who eats bread", a composite from ἄλφι (flour) and the suffix -ησ-τής, "eater", and by extension meant "one who earns his daily bread, his food", therefore "the worker" and consequently "the active, the industrious"
